Qonshu CRM

Partner App API — Data Requirements

What the CRM needs from the mobile-app backend

Version 1.0  ·  17 July 2026  ·  For: Backend team (Django)

1. Overview

Qonshu CRM shows an Analytics view for every partner company that uses the mobile app. To populate it, the CRM sends a partner and a date range, and expects that partner's numbers for that range in return.

We sendPartner company
Date from → Date to
→
You returnUser counts, installations, payment totals & breakdowns, and the payments list
Read-only. The CRM never writes to your system. One endpoint is enough.

2. Request

GET /api/qonshu/partner-stats?partner=Acme+Towers&date_from=2026-07-01&date_to=2026-07-31
ParameterExampleNotes
partnerAcme TowersThe partner company. If you have a stable partner id, we'd rather send that than the name.
date_from2026-07-01Start of the range (inclusive).
date_to2026-07-31End of the range (inclusive).

3. Response

A. Users

FieldTypeMeaning
total_usersnumberAll app users of this partner.
active_usersnumberUsers currently active in your system.
engaged_usersnumberUsers who made at least one payment within the date range.
total_debtnumberTotal amount currently owed by this partner's users.

B. Installations

FieldTypeMeaning
total_installationsnumberHow many users installed the app.
ios_installationsnumberInstalled on iOS.
android_installationsnumberInstalled on Android.
logged_in_usersnumberInstalled and logged in at least once — this is how we measure real usage, not just downloads.

C. Payments in the date range

FieldTypeMeaning
total_payments_countnumberHow many payments were made.
total_payments_amountnumberTotal money paid.
amount_by_cardnumberOf that total — paid by card.
amount_by_manualnumberOf that total — recorded manually.
amount_by_cashnumberOf that total — paid in cash.
utility_payments_countnumberHow many utility payments.
utility_payments_amountnumberTotal utility amount.
apartment_payments_amountnumberTotal for apartments.
parking_payments_amountnumberTotal for parking.
non_residential_payments_amountnumberTotal for non-residential units.
The three method amounts (card + manual + cash) should add up to total_payments_amount. The four category amounts (utility + apartment + parking + non-residential) should also add up to it.

D. Payments list

The individual payments in the range, so the CRM can show a transactions table.

FieldTypeMeaning
idstringYour id for the payment.
user_idstringWho paid.
user_namestringTheir name (or a label, if names can't be shared).
datedatetimeWhen the payment happened.
amountnumberHow much.
methodenumCARD · MANUAL · CASH
categoryenumAPARTMENT · PARKING · NON_RESIDENTIAL · UTILITY

5. Format notes

TopicRule
DatesISO format — 2026-07-01 for the range, 2026-07-14T09:21:00Z for timestamps.
MoneyNumber with 2 decimals — 149.90.
No dataReturn the same structure with 0s and an empty payments list — not an error.
Long listsIf payments is very long, paginate it — but keep the totals complete for the whole range.

6. Please confirm

  1. Do the method values (card / manual / cash) and category values (apartment / parking / non-residential / utility) match your system? If you have more, please send the full list.
  2. Can you provide logged_in_users — users who installed and logged in? This is our key usage metric.
  3. How do you handle refunds / cancelled payments — excluded, or sent as negative amounts?
  4. Is there a partner id we should send instead of the company name?
  5. Can you also expose a list of partners (id + name), so we can match them to our records automatically?
